      ## Layout Management

      ### Key principle

      **A layout is a UI composition decision, not a business one.**
      > It describes _how_ things are displayed, not _what_ is done.

      ### Types of layouts and where they belong
      <br />

      #### 1. Page (route) layout
      *   Wraps the entire page
      *   Chosen by the routing layer

      đź“Ť **In** **`pages/`**
      *   Global <ArrowRightIcon size={16} className="inline-block" /> directly in `_app.tsx`, or extract **UI** into `layouts/defaultLayout.tsx` if the file becomes too large and UI elements hurt readability
      *   Shared contextual <ArrowRightIcon size={16} className="inline-block" /> `shared/components/layouts/`

      #### 2. Shared layout (non-business)
      *   Used by multiple pages
      *   Independent from business domains

      đź“Ť **`shared/components/layouts/`**

      Examples: EditorLayout, DashboardLayout, FullWidthLayout, CenteredLayout, ...

      #### 3. Sub-layout (partial layout)
      <br />

      ##### a) Business
      *   Structures a UI section tied to a domain
      *   Reused across multiple screens

      đź“Ť **`features/*/`**

      ##### b) Generic
      *   Reusable UI structure
      *   No business relation

      đź“Ť **`shared/components/layouts/`**

      ### What NOT to do
      *   âťŚ Layout inside a feature if it is decided by the route
      *   âťŚ Business layout inside `shared/`

      ### Quick rule
      The **route** selects the **layout**
      > The **feature** provides the **business UI**
      > **Business sub-layouts** stay within the **feature**
